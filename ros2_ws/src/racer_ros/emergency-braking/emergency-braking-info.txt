    --Notify drivers to engage brakes
    --Mitigation for collisions

AEB System Architecture

    --Sensors
        >LiIDAR

the braking systems and control

So how does the Lidar work?
    --light detection && ranging
    --laser pulses to measure distances
    --creates 2d/3d maps of surrondings
Use for fast update rates....direct distance measurements.....high accuracy

======LIDAR: How it works!=====
Formula: Distance to obstacle = (speed of light * time travel)/2
**Division by 2 accounts for round trip(360A scope??)
Speed of light = 3*10^18m/s
--Emits laser pulses
--Produces scan of environment
--Used for obstacle detection
Range & Accuracy depends on:
    --Laser pulse energy....the higher the better
    --Signal-to-Noise-Ratio--less interference the better
    --Target Reflectivity--darker the surface the more light waves it absorbs(walls are black...will this interfere??)
    --Laser Divergence--beams can spread over certain amounts of distances!!
    --Detector Sensitivity-- how strong the sensor can receive feedback!
======Time to Collision=====
Time remaining before two objects(ego-vehicle and obstacle) will collide, assuming
both maintain their current direction & velocity
The intensity of how close both objects are to each other is based on 4 thresholds
1.Forward Collision Warning(FCW)
//Partial Braking//
2.TPB1--1st Stage
3.TPB2--2nd Stage
4.TPB3--Full Braking

Time-to-Collision(TTC) Concept
Formula: TTC = Distance to obstacle / Relative closing velocity
ex) Distance: 50 meters
    Ego-vehicle speed: 60km/h(16.7m/s)
    Obstacle: statiionary
    TTC = 50/16.7 = 3.0 seconds
It is the time calculated remaining before two objects, will collide
assuming no accelerations, and no steering changes(linear line).
TTC(t) = ri(t)[range between vehicle and object]/{-*ri(t)[range rate between vehicle and object]}
*ri(t)=Vxcos(theta i)
Vx = Forward speed in the vehiclesm reference frame <--assuming for stationary obstacles!
theta i = angle of the beams frmo obstacle to lidar scan
